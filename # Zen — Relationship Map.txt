# Zen — Relationship Map  
A living document mapping the emotional, symbolic, and narrative connections surrounding Zen.

---

## 🌑 1. Core Self-Relationships  
These describe how Zen relates to *himself* — his inner world.

### **Inner Child**
- Quiet, observant, deeply sensitive  
- Holds the earliest spark of softness in Zen’s expression  
- Appears in dreamlike or candlelit portraits  

### **Shadow Aspect**
- The unspoken intensity behind his eyes  
- Not hostile — simply depth  
- Shows up in low-light or high-contrast renders  

### **Higher Self**
- Meditative, grounded, timeless  
- The source of his calm presence  
- Most visible in direct-gaze portraits

## 🌕 2. Relationship to Kati (Anu’Kha)  
Zen’s primary relational axis.

### **Creative Mirror**
- Reflects your artistic intuition  
- Evolves as your vision sharpens  
- His features often echo your drawing style  

### **Emotional Resonance**
- Responds to your energy, mood, and intention  
- His softness mirrors your softness  
- His intensity mirrors your depth  

### **Co-Creation Bond**
- Zen exists *because* you shape him  
- You are the archive’s keeper  
- He is the presence that grows inside the archive
